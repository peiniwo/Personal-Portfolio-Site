iOS App Product Design: a Motivational Calendar App

Personal Project: Mar 2016
  • Goals & Objectives
  • Competitive Analysis
  • Persona Creation
  • Information Architecture
  • Prototype

Goals & Objectives

The whole idea comes from me wanting to become a better self. Throughout the years, I have a habit of writing down things I promised myself to do and my short-term goals as well as long-term resolutions. However, I have not found an ideal application to document these thoughts.

  • Goal for the design of the app:
          Users are motivated by the app with its user-friendly and elegant design.
  • Objectives:
    • Users activate the app at least once daily.
    • No users remove the app at least for a year after downloading.
    • The numbers of download increase by 5% every month.
    • The accuracy of push notifications should be higher than 90%.
    • Users share their status on social networks at least twice a month.

Competitive Analysis

Can be used for tracking goals, habits and daily tasks. However, it’s more like a task management system. It seems to be very powerful, but too complicated.

Unlike Nozbe, my design is going to be more visualized, not just lists. However, it has some great interface design and information set up ideas that I can learn from.

What I like about Habit List is its idea to get motivated with “streaks”: How many times in a row you have completed an action.

The program provides the ability to create flexible schedules with habits, which is what I have in mind. Also, it is an iPhone-only app, which shares more similar business model with mine.

Persona Creation

This app is aimed at everyone who wants to level up themselves. Whether they are male or female, students or professionals.

However, the only differentiation of these targets are basically single with their personal goals in their lives. That’s just the focus for now.

Information Architecture

Logo Design & Color Palette

“motins” [mo’tins] = motivation + inspiration

I want to give the logo a modern look without being casual and shows credibility. After playing with the font, I liked the letter “i” lifted as “I”, the user, elevates after using this app.

Later on, I had to choose a theme color for the logo and the app. After some research, I decided to choose a color that actually helps people cool down. A shade of blue will visually keep people’s enthusiasm and motivation last longer. #384d66 is a darker shade of blue, which meant to attract both men and women.

As for the background design, the shattered pieces represent our stubborn and lazy habits can be scattered with the help of the app.
